FreshChain PDF revision notes

Objective
Rewrite the PDF as a FreshChain product/process overview rather than a document that talks about a demo. The visual structure remains, but the language now describes the product, the operating model, and the evidence produced by the system.

Language direction
Remove meta wording such as global demo brief, prepared for a global audience, talk track, judges, panel flow, and phrases that explain the document itself. Use product language instead:

- FreshChain cold-chain rescue process
- Cold-chain rescue and protected-value control on SAP BTP
- Operating model
- Incident control
- Risk scoring
- Store execution
- Financial traceability
- FreshChain operating sequence

Visual direction
Keep the existing clean card layout and screenshot-led pages. Shift the dominant palette from blue/green to red, white, charcoal, and light grey so the document aligns better with retailsolutions SA and Shoprite branding. Keep green only for actual positive status and protected-value signals.

Content direction
The PDF should explain the product in terms of the process FreshChain runs:

1. Detect the cold-chain exception.
2. Persist the reading with store, zone, product, sensor, and stock context.
3. Score spoilage risk through SAP AI Core.
4. Build a rescue plan from affected stock, recommendation, SLA, and rescue economics.
5. Execute the store action and record workflow completion proof.
6. Calculate protected value from stock ledger, risk, confidence, and salvage cap.
7. Monitor recurring risk, ingestion quality, and intervention outcomes.

Financial explanation
The protected-value page remains central. It explains that stock at risk comes from active stock in the affected zone, expected loss is stock value x AI risk x AI confidence, the salvage cap comes from configured rescue economics, and protected value is the lower of expected loss and salvage cap.
